01SQL · Power BIPersonal project

Freelance Skill Demand & Pricing Analysis

the problem: a raw freelance job postings dataset, messy currency symbols, inconsistent experience-level labels, and no clear read on which skills actually pay or where demand is concentrated.

what i built: cleaned and transformed the dataset in SQL, stripping currency symbols, casting types, and standardizing categorical fields. Used CTEs, window functions, and CASE WHEN bucketing to analyze pricing tiers and skill demand, then built an interactive Power BI dashboard with KPIs, slicers, and drill-through filters.

SQLCTEsWindow FunctionsPower BI
result: found that intermediate-level roles dominate postings, CRM and business skills command higher rates than expected, and job volume is concentrated in a small number of countries.

02Python · PandasPersonal project

Telecom Network Usage Analysis (Milan CDR Dataset)

the problem: ~15 million call detail records from the Telecom Italia Big Data Challenge, with missing values, inconsistent timestamps, and no aggregated view of where and when the network was under strain.

what i built: processed the full dataset using Python (Pandas, NumPy), handling missing values, timestamp conversion, and aggregation at scale. Ran time-series and cell-tower load analysis to surface peak traffic windows and congestion-prone towers, then visualized hourly and daily patterns with Matplotlib and Seaborn heatmaps.

PythonPandasNumPySeaborn
result: identified evening hours as the highest-usage window and a small subset of towers carrying disproportionate load — plus a strong correlation between call, SMS, and internet usage relevant to capacity planning.

03SQL · PythonPersonal project

EV & E-Rickshaw Adoption Trends Analysis (India)

the problem: ten years (2015–2025) of national EV sales data sourced from Kaggle, spanning vehicle categories and segments, with no clear story yet on what's actually driving adoption.

what i built: cleaned the dataset in SQL and ran year-on-year growth and EV penetration trend analysis in Python, then built category-wise sales comparison charts to communicate adoption momentum to a non-technical audience.

SQLPythonMatplotlibSeaborn
result: consistent multi-year growth in EV adoption, driven primarily by 2-wheelers and 3-wheelers rather than passenger cars.
